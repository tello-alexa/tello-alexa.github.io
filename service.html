Past Service

I spent three years in high school assisting teachers during my lunch period by helping them grade papers, organize or clean classrooms after dealing with notably rambunctious students. Eventually, I was accepted into my high school's chapter of the National Honor Society, where we were focused on supporting people in our community by hosting various events throughout the year. Below are some of the most memorable ones:

  • Teacher's Pageant - a traditional pageant competition between teachers where all funds from the event were donated to local veterans.
  • Holiday Party - an annual event for special education students and their families to celebrate the holidays with games and treats.
  • Trunk-or-Treat - a safe environment for families to trick-or-treat that also acted as a fundraiser to fight homelessness.

Another organization I've worked with is For Love & Art, non-profit that allows people with limited mobility to have the art gallery experience from the comfort of their homes. It was because of my volunteering with this organization that I became fond of the elderly as I visited retirement homes and individual families to look through and discuss art.

Hopeful Service

Last year, my quarantine activity of choice became knitting after messing with my sister's starter kit she hadn't touched, but since then it's become a bit of a hobby of mine. Because of this, I am hoping to contribute to Aggie Knitting, Crafting, and More (Aggie KCAM), a community service organization that helps the Bryan-College Station area by making scarves, gloves and other apparel for those in need. I am hoping that during my senior year that I can be a part of Big Event, the largest student-run service project in the United States and hosted by Texas A&M University aiming to show students' appreciation for the community by helping out local residents.
